# Kasa Competitive Notes — Tattoo Intake

## The landscape (what artists use today)
| Tool | What it is | Why it's weak for tattoo intake |
|---|---|---|
| **Google Forms** | Free generic form | Ugly, clinical, no images-first UX, no intelligence, raw responses to read |
| **Jotform / Cognito / Typeform** | Form builders | Prettier than Google but still generic, per-form pricing, no tattoo brain, artist still reads raw answers |
| **Instagram DMs** | Native | No structure — artist re-asks the same 10 questions every time; chaos to triage |
| **Acuity / Square / GlossGenius / Vagaro** | Booking + scheduling suites | Booking-first; intake is a thin afterthought; force the artist onto their whole system; not tattoo-shaped |
| **Tattoo-specific (e.g. studio CRMs, "Tattoo Booking" apps)** | Niche booking tools | Often heavy/studio-oriented, dated UX, lock-in; intake quality still low |

## The gap Kasa fills
Everyone collects data; **nobody turns it into a brief.** The artist's real pain
isn't "I need a form" — it's "after the form, I still have to read messy answers,
open 6 images, figure out what's missing, and decide." Kasa owns the **post-
submission intelligence + triage**, which none of the above do.

## Positioning
- Primary: **"Stop reading messy tattoo forms. Get artist-ready briefs instead."**
- Alts: "Turn tattoo requests into artist-ready briefs." · "A smarter tattoo
  request form." · "AI intake for tattoo artists." · "From Instagram inquiry to
  clean brief."

## Why we win on the wedge
1. **Beauty** — the instant a client taps the link, Kasa looks better than any
   form. Visible differentiation, zero explanation needed.
2. **Intelligence** — the AI brief is the thing no competitor has. It's the "aha."
3. **No lock-in** — we DON'T replace their booking/deposit system (MVP). Lowest
   switching cost: keep Acuity/Venmo/DMs, just swap the *form*. "Useful even if
   you still book manually."
4. **Tattoo-shaped** — body placement, size comparisons, reference categorization,
   flash/custom/cover-up, AI-art flagging, deposit/age/consent. Generic forms
   can't express this; booking suites don't bother.

## Strategic risks / honest watch-outs
- **"Just a form" perception** → must lead with the BRIEF (intelligence), not the
  form, in all messaging + the demo.
- **Low switching cost cuts both ways** — easy to adopt, easy to drop. Stickiness
  comes from the dashboard becoming where they triage (and later: templates, DM
  auto-reply, deposits).
- **Booking suites could add AI intake** — but they're incentivized to lock you
  into their whole stack; our neutrality (works alongside anything) is the moat
  for solo artists who refuse to switch systems.
- **AI quality bar** — a wrong brief is worse than no brief (trust). Conservative
  AI behavior (AI_BEHAVIOR.md) is a competitive necessity, not just polish.

## Pricing hypothesis (validate — see VALIDATION_PLAN.md)

Land as "the beautiful smart request form" → become the artist's triage home →
then add deposits (Stripe), DM auto-reply with the link, templates, calendar.
Each expansion increases stickiness without forcing a system switch up front.
